A DRIP (Dividend Reinvestment Plan) automatically uses your dividend payments to buy more shares instead of taking the cash. Over time, this compounds your returns — more shares means more dividends, which buys even more shares. Enter your starting investment, expected annual yield, and time horizon to see how reinvesting compares to taking dividends as cash. Even a modest 3–5% yield reinvested over 20 years can dramatically outperform a non-DRIP portfolio.
